Solar System Overview

The solar system consists of the Sun and everything that orbits around it, including planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and meteoroids. It extends from the Sun at the center to the outer edges of the Kuiper Belt.

Planets of the Solar System

Mercury

The closest planet to the Sun, Mercury has a very thin atmosphere and experiences extreme temperatures.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is known for its thick, toxic atmosphere and high temperatures.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and is the only known planet to support life.

Mars

Mars, the fourth planet, is known as the Red Planet due to its reddish appearance, caused by iron oxide on its surface.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, known for its Great Red Spot and many moons.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its beautiful ring system, which is made up of ice and rock particles.

Uranus

Uranus is known for its blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere and its unique sideways rotation.

Neptune

Neptune, the eighth planet, is known for its deep blue color and strong winds, the fastest in the solar system.

Famous Stars in the Night Sky

Sirius

Sirius, also known as the Dog Star, is the brightest star in the Earth's night sky. It is part of the constellation Canis Major.

Betelgeuse

Betelgeuse is a red supergiant star in the constellation Orion. It is one of the largest visible stars and is nearing the end of its life.

Vega

Vega is the brightest star in the constellation Lyra and the fifth-brightest star in the night sky. It is about 25 light-years from Earth.

Polaris

Polaris, also known as the North Star, is the brightest star in the constellation Ursa Minor. It is directly above the Earth's North Pole.

Altair

Altair is the brightest star in the constellation Aquila and one of the closest stars visible to the naked eye.

Antares

Antares is the brightest star in the constellation Scorpius and is a red supergiant nearing the end of its life cycle.

Procyon

Procyon is the brightest star in the constellation Canis Minor and one of the nearest stars to the Sun.

Rigel

Rigel is the brightest star in the constellation Orion and one of the most luminous stars in our galaxy.
